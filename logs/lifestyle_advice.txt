Here are some Indian breakfast options suitable for diabetics, including portion sizes, preparation methods, and meal timing:

- **Vegetable Daliya (Broken Wheat Porridge)**
  - **Ingredients and Portion Sizes:**
    - 1 cup broken wheat (daliya)
    - 1/2 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, beans, peas)
    - 1 tsp ghee
    - Spices (turmeric, cumin seeds, salt to taste)
  - **Preparation Method:**
    - Heat ghee in a pan and roast the broken wheat until golden brown.
    - Add mixed vegetables and sauté for a few minutes.
    - Add 2 cups of water, spices, and cook until the daliya is soft and well-cooked.
  - **Meal Timing:**
    - Ideal for breakfast between 7 AM and 9 AM.
  - **Weekly Frequency:**
    - 2-3 times a week.

- **Moong Dal Chilla (Savory Pancakes)**
  - **Ingredients and Portion Sizes:**
    - 1/2 cup soaked moong dal
    - 1 small onion, finely chopped
    - 1 green chili, finely chopped
    - Spices (cumin seeds, salt to taste)
  - **Preparation Method:**
    - Grind soaked moong dal into a smooth batter.
    - Mix in chopped onions, green chili, and spices.
    - Pour small portions of the batter onto a non-stick pan and cook until golden brown on both sides.
  - **Meal Timing:**
    - Ideal for breakfast between 7 AM and 9 AM.
  - **Weekly Frequency:**
    - 2-3 times a week.

- **Besan Chilla (Gram Flour Pancakes)**
  - **Ingredients and Portion Sizes:**
    - 1 cup besan (gram flour)
    - 1 small onion, finely chopped
    - 1 green chili, finely chopped
    - Spices (turmeric, cumin seeds, salt to taste)
  - **Preparation Method:**
    - Mix besan with water to form a smooth batter.
    - Add chopped onions, green chili, and spices.
    - Pour small portions of the batter onto a non-stick pan and cook until golden brown on both sides.
  - **Meal Timing:**
    - Ideal for breakfast between 7 AM and 9 AM.
  - **Weekly Frequency:**
    - 2-3 times a week.

- **Vegetable Oats Upma**
  - **Ingredients and Portion Sizes:**
    - 1/2 cup oats
    - 1/4 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, beans, peas)
    - 1 tsp oil
    - Spices (mustard seeds, turmeric, salt to taste)
  - **Preparation Method:**
    - Heat oil in a pan and add mustard seeds.
    - Add mixed vegetables and sauté for a few minutes.
    - Add oats, 1 cup of water, spices, and cook until the oats are soft and well-cooked.
  - **Meal Timing:**
    - Ideal for breakfast between 7 AM and 9 AM.
  - **Weekly Frequency:**
    - 2-3 times a week.

- **Sprouted Salad**
  - **Ingredients and Portion Sizes:**
    - 3/4 cup mixed sprouts (moong, chana)
    - 1 small cucumber, finely chopped
    - 1 small tomato, finely chopped
    - Lemon juice and salt to taste
  - **Preparation Method:**
    - Boil the mixed sprouts until tender.
    - Mix with chopped cucumber, tomato, lemon juice, and salt.
  - **Meal Timing:**
    - Ideal for breakfast between 7 AM and 9 AM.
  - **Weekly Frequency:**
    - 2-3 times a week.

- **Idli/Sambar**
  - **Ingredients and Portion Sizes:**
    - 2 idlis (made from low-glycemic rice and lentils)
    - 1 cup sambar (made from toor dal, vegetables, and sambar powder)
  - **Preparation Method:**
    - Prepare idli batter using low-glycemic rice and lentils, ferment overnight, and steam cook.
    - Prepare sambar by cooking toor dal with vegetables and sambar powder.
  - **Meal Timing:**
    - Ideal for breakfast between 7 AM and 9 AM.
  - **Weekly Frequency:**
    - 2 times a week.

- **Vegetable Poha**
  - **Ingredients and Portion Sizes:**
    - 1 cup poha (flattened rice)
    - 1/4 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, beans, peas)
    - 1 tsp oil
    - Spices (mustard seeds, turmeric, salt to taste)
  - **Preparation Method:**
    - Heat oil in a pan and add mustard seeds.
    - Add mixed vegetables and sauté for a few minutes.
    - Add poha, spices, and cook for a few minutes until well-mixed.
  - **Meal Timing:**
    - Ideal for breakfast between 7 AM and 9 AM.
  - **Weekly Frequency:**
    - 2-3 times a week.
